Social Media Intern

You hustle. You learn. You convert.

Noida (Sector 119, in-office) 3 months → permanent 9 AM - 6 PM Current students welcome

About the role

We run content for some of India's biggest creators and brands. Raj Shamani, Badshah, BJP, and more. 450M+ views in the last 6 months. 100M+ views every month right now.

The Social Media Intern is the cross-functional support seat. You spend roughly 40% editing, 30% writing captions and ideas, and 30% on social media management — posting, scheduling, DM triage.

This is a 3-month internship with stipend. If you hit the bar at the 3-month mark, you convert to a permanent role on the team.

Title path: Social Media Intern → Social Media Executive → Content Associate.

What you will own

  • Basic reel editing from team-shot raw footage. 2-3 publishable reels per day from raw to finished.
  • Story templates for Instagram, designed and scheduled across all client + agency pages.
  • Caption + hashtag + CTA writing for upcoming posts, in the founder voice doc.
  • Posting + scheduling across Instagram, YouTube Shorts, Facebook (Buffer / Later / Meta Suite).
  • DM triage + comment engagement. Sort inbound into categories. Respond to engagement comments within 4 hours.
  • Idea support. Bring 3-5 fresh hook ideas or trending audio finds to the daily sync.
  • Asset organization across Drive and Notion.
  • Weekly performance tracking. Pull stats into a simple Notion dashboard every Friday.

Who we are looking for

  • Basic video editing fluency in any tool (CapCut, Premiere, DaVinci, Canva for Video).
  • Content native. You watch reels for fun, not just for work. You know what's trending right now.
  • Hustle. When today's list is done you find the next thing without being asked.
  • Coachable. Feedback lands, defensiveness does not.
  • Internet fluent. Memes, formats, current creators — you can name them.
  • Hungry for the permanent role. An intern who treats this as a stepping stone grows into the seat.

Current students or recent grads are welcome. Any field of study is fine.

This role is not for you if

  • You wait for instructions when there is no clear task.
  • You do not actually use Instagram or cannot name a current trend.
  • You are only here for the stipend, not the permanent-role pathway.
  • You prefer "structured environments" over a fast-moving small team.
  • You get defensive when feedback is harsh.
